# Standard Playbook Member App — Onboarding Recording Script

## Purpose

Record one master orientation and five short contextual chapters using a demo member with invented data. The in-app `HelpButton` entries use the same chapter names, so the finished clips can be attached without rewriting the product.

The single idea every recording must reinforce:

> Quarterly sets direction. This Month names the focus. Weekly schedules the work. Daily records the proof.

Do not say that every handoff is automatic. Quarterly can seed empty This Month slots. Members explicitly send a monthly mission to the Weekly Bench and choose the daily proof they want shown in Daily.

## Demo account

Use a fictitious member named **Alex Morgan**. Hide browser tabs, bookmarks, email addresses, and route URLs.

Use believable demo content:

- Body target: Complete three strength sessions every week through the quarter.
- Being target: Read scripture before opening the phone every weekday.
- Balance target: Protect one device-free family evening every week.
- Business target: Complete and launch the producer onboarding playbook.
- Current Business mission: Finish the first complete onboarding draft.
- Weekly measurable: Complete one operating section and schedule its review.
- Daily Business proof: Write the next onboarding section for 30 focused minutes.
- Weekly Bench item: Finish the first onboarding draft.
- One Big Thing: Review and approve the complete onboarding playbook.

## Master orientation — 6:30

### 0:00–0:35 — The big idea

**Screen:** Hub, cadence map visible.

**Voiceover:**

“Standard Playbook is not another place to store goals. It is a rhythm that carries the long view into the day in front of you. Quarterly gives you direction. This Month gives that direction a focus. Weekly turns the focus into scheduled plays. Daily records the proof.”

**Action:** Move the pointer across the cadence map from Quarterly to Daily.

### 0:35–1:15 — The Hub

**Screen:** Hub with setup guide, Today’s Rhythm, and populated quarterly/monthly cards.

**Voiceover:**

“The Hub is your current operating view. It does not create a second set of goals. It surfaces the work you defined elsewhere and points to the next useful move. New members can use the setup guide once, dismiss it, and reopen it from any page’s help button.”

**Action:** Expand and collapse the setup guide. Point to the question-mark help control.

### 1:15–2:55 — Quarterly direction

**Screen:** Quarterly landing, then Brain Dump, Choose Your Targets, Review Quarterly Direction, and Plan the Next 3 Months.

**Voiceover:**

“Start Quarterly with possibilities, not perfect wording. Add at least one idea in Body, Being, Balance, and Business. Choose the targets that actually belong in this quarter. On the review screen, add context or make a final edit. The clarity check is optional. Saving builds a three-month mission plan.”

“If you keep two targets in a domain, choose the Main Focus. That is the direction that can feed This Month, Daily proof, and 90 Day Audio.”

**Action:** Add an idea, use an edit tooltip, choose targets, save, and show the three monthly suggestion cards.

### 2:55–3:45 — This Month

**Screen:** This Month with four populated missions.

**Voiceover:**

“This Month is the live execution layer. Quarterly may seed an empty slot, but anything you edit here remains yours. Give each domain one mission and one weekly measurable. When a mission is ready to become scheduled work, send it to the Weekly Bench.”

**Action:** Edit the Business mission, then click **Send to Weekly**.

### 3:45–4:40 — Weekly

**Screen:** Weekly Playbook with the newly sent Bench item.

**Voiceover:**

“The Bench is the handoff point between focus and execution. Schedule an item onto a day as a Power Play, or make the single most meaningful result your One Big Thing. On desktop you can drag. On any device you can use the visible schedule control.”

**Action:** Schedule the Business mission to Tuesday, then show One Big Thing.

### 4:40–5:20 — Daily proof

**Screen:** Daily/Core Four with quarterly proof text beneath the four domain tiles.

**Voiceover:**

“Daily is not asking whether you had a perfect day. It asks whether you completed the action you committed to in each domain. Selected quarterly proof appears beneath the matching tile. Complete the action, then mark the domain. Each completed domain adds one point.”

**Action:** Hover the Business tile, then mark it complete.

### 5:20–6:05 — Coach and review

**Screen:** Flows, Weekly Reflection, and Debrief.

**Voiceover:**

“When life happens, use a Flow to work through the moment and leave with a move. Weekly Reflection finds themes across the Flows you completed. Debrief closes the week, creates course corrections, and sets the next One Big Thing.”

**Action:** Open a Flow info tooltip, then show the Reflection and Debrief help controls.

### 6:05–6:30 — Reinforce and get help

**Screen:** 90 Day Audio, then a page help dialog.

**Voiceover:**

“Your main quarterly targets can also become a personal 90 Day Audio track. Wherever you are in the app, use the question-mark control for what the page is for, what its buttons do, and where it fits in the operating rhythm.”

## Contextual help clips

Record these as independent 60–90 second chapters:

1. `core4_page` — **How Daily and the 56-point score work**
2. `weekly_playbook` — **Planning your week with the Bench and Power Plays**
3. `tool-quarterly-targets` — **Building a quarter that reaches today**
4. `flows-overview` — **Choosing and completing your first Flow**
5. `The_Debrief` — **Closing the week with Debrief**
6. `monthly_missions` — **Turning a quarterly plan into this month’s focus**
7. `weekly_reflection` — **Reading your Weekly Reflection**
8. `theta_audio` — **Creating your first 90 Day Audio track**

Each clip should:

- begin with “Use this page when…”;
- demonstrate one real action;
- explain one consequence-heavy control;
- end with the next place in the cadence;
- stay below 90 seconds;
- use captions and a visible pointer highlight;
- avoid real member goals, reflections, or account data.

## Capture checklist

- Desktop: 1440 × 1000, browser chrome cropped.
- Mobile: modern iPhone portrait for Daily, Flow, and the navigation drawer.
- Light mode for the master recording; capture one short dark-mode insert.
- Populate all four domains before recording.
- Keep the same demo content across every clip.
- Pause after each click so captions and pointer movement remain readable.
- Record the final UI only after the deployed build matches the local build.
